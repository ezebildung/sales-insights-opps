Sales Insights

Week of June 22, 2026 · 5 calls reviewed

Insights

Use Case Transactional push (card swipe notifications) is the primary driver for fintech/benefits leads upgrading from free plans.
Use Case Abandoned cart / incomplete loan application journeys are the #1 automation use case for LATAM fintech prospects.
Objection Price shock when leads see the self-serve site and compare to the negotiated annual contract price — the gap feels too large.
Objection Internal budget approval process (comité, directores) creates delays and uncertainty in closing — common in structured companies.
Competitor Salesforce Marketing Cloud is entrenched in some accounts (Proa) — unpredictable add-on costs are the main pain vs. OneSignal.
Doubt MAU billing model is frequently misunderstood — leads confuse "monthly active users" with total registered users or first-time users.
Other WhatsApp blocking issues (Meta BM bans, no direct Meta partnership via Twilio) are a recurring deal blocker for BR prospects.

Opportunities & Learnings

When showing the self-serve pricing page, proactively frame the contract discount before the lead does the math themselves — it lands better when you control the narrative.
Leads on free plans often don't know which features they're missing. An early "free plan audit" in the discovery can generate urgency and appreciation for the upgrade value.
For deals going to committee approval, send a one-pager with ROI framing (not just feature lists) to help the internal champion sell it upward.
The Bildung services pitch should come earlier in the demo — not just at pricing time. Position it as part of the value from the start to reduce sticker shock at close.

Product Gaps

No native Meta Business Partner relationship — WhatsApp via Twilio is a recurring friction point and deal risk, especially for BR accounts.
Tags are not labeled as "custom fields" in the UI — leads with CRM backgrounds find the mental model confusing and expect field-level semantics.
No Growth plan for accounts under $25K MAU threshold on Pro — pricing gap leaves a dead zone between Growth and Pro for small but high-volume accounts.
Free plan deprecation timeline (Oct 1) creates urgency but also anxiety — no self-serve migration path or grace-period tools for existing free accounts.

Sales Insights

Week of June 15, 2026 · 6 calls reviewed

Insights

Competitor Infobip named as direct alternative in two BR fintech calls; one lead actively evaluating both.
Use Case Omnichannel debt collection (push, email, SMS escalation) is a primary driver for fintech leads.
Use Case Habit creation for D1–D7 users is the core retention goal across fitness and short-video apps.
Doubt MAU definition recurs across calls — leads assume it counts messaged users, not app openers.
Objection Growth→Pro plan gap is too wide; leads hitting 3-journey limit don't see a natural intermediate step.

Opportunities & Learnings

WhatsApp is a blocker in BR market — team needs a sharper, ready answer on the Twilio path and limitations before it comes up.
MAU definition should be proactively explained before pricing, not reactively after confusion arises.
Event Streams (data export) addresses a recurring analytics objection — it should be part of the standard pitch, not an afterthought.
Infobip comparisons come up without a prepared counternarrative; a direct differentiation talking point would help close faster.

Product Gaps

No native WhatsApp channel; Twilio dependency adds friction and cost, especially for BR/LATAM market.
No aggregated journey-level conversion reporting; leads expect a single funnel view, not per-message metrics.
Tags behave as key-value pairs, not structured custom fields; confusing for leads coming from Salesforce or HubSpot.
Only one identity resolution method (Login); no alias support limits anonymous-to-known user tracking flexibility.
